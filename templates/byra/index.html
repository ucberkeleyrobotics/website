ABOUT US



The Overview

Fundamentals of Robotics, developed and taught by volunteers from the UC Berkeley student group Robotics@Berkeley, explores the science and technology behind robotic systems through practical, hands-on projects.

The Specifics

The program is designed to interest students in the field of robotics and motivate them to pursue an advanced education in science and engineering. In this class, students will apply learn the fundamentals of computer programming, electronics, and system controls necessary to develop a robotic system. Using the engineering design team concept as a model, students work in small groups to design, program, and construct robotic devices.

The Topics

The course of study includes Design Process, Electricity, Arduino Programming, Machine Control, and Robotic System Design. Assignments and course documents will be available via Google Drive. All students will be given access to the materials prior to the beginning of the course.

CURRICULUM



This course will be taught in six two-hour sessions on the UC Berkeley campus in XXX Cory Hall. The course has a single $120 fee to pay for the components of the robot systems that each student will develop and keep -- all funds that donâ€™t go toward materials will be donated directly to Robotics@Berkeley to support UC Berkeley student projects. At each session there will be one primary adult instructor leading the course and at least two assistant instructors. Additional, online lectures will be made for students who wish to further explore robotics outside the class time.

The general session format will be a short lecture followed by a longer hands-on project period, where students apply what they learned in their own robotic system they are developing. Each student will maintain an engineering notebook, in which they will take notes on new information and document the development of their robotic system. Students will work in pairs on all project components, but they will each develop their own robot. The course will culminate in a competition in which students program their robots to complete a maze as quickly as possible.
