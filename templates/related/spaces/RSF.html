Descriptions:

The Richmond Field Station (RFS) is an academic facility located outside of the immediate Berkeley campus. The 170-acre property containing 100 acres of uplands with the remainder being marsh/bay lands has been used for large-scale engineering research since 1950. The 500,000 square feet of research space in the RFS includes one of the world’s largest earthquake shaking tables, test facilities for advanced transportation research, a laboratory for the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency, and the Northern Regional Library Facility which archives 7.7 million lesser-used books for 4 other UC campuses. The RFS is accessible to UC Berkeley labs and student groups who do need more space for testing than is allowed on campus. Many of the larger engineering projects done at UC Berkeley are tested at the RFS.
