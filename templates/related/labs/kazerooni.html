Berkeley Robotics and Human Engineering Laboratory


Website: bleex.me.berkeley.edu

Lab Director: Dr. Homayoon Kazerooni - kazerooni [at] berkeley.edu

Description:

As stated on his lab’s website, Dr. Kazerooni conducts research on “robotics, control sciences, exoskeletons, human-machine systems and augmentation, bioengineering, mechatronics design, artificial locomotion, intelligent assist devices, and power and propulsion.” His work aims to lower the mobility and object transportation issues that humans encounter.

Berkeley Lower Extremity Exoskeleton (BLEEX), ExoHiker™, and ExoClimber™ were three of his notable exoskeletons projects that eventually lead to the creation of Human Universal Load Carrier (HULC), a energetically-independent lower exoskeleton allowed users to relocate 200-pound loads regardless of terrain. It includes features from ExoHiker™ and ExoClimber™. A video of HULC can be found here.

In previous work focusing instead on upper extremities, his team created a new class of intelligent assistive devices now being used worldwide to assist workers with manipulations of heavy devices, which can be found here.
