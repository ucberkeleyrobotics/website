Model Predictive Control Lab


Website: mpc.berkeley.edu

Contact: Professor Francesco Borrelli - fborrelli [at] me.berkeley.edu

Description:

In the words of their website, “Our research lab focuses on the theoretical and real-time implementation aspects of constrained predictive model-based control… We deal with a wide range of systems in the automotive field, in the process industries and in robotics, including several full scale industrial problems.” For more information, see this video of Professor Borrelli explaining the topics of research in the MPC Lab.

Autonomous Vehicles - Partnered with Hyundai, researchers at the MPC Lab explore the potential of Model Predictive Control in both semi-autonomous and fully autonomous vehicle control. They explore autonomous vehicle controls in applications such as parking, changing lanes, and driving on slippery surfaces; they have several autonomous vehicles for testing at the Richmond Field Station and at a larger autonomous vehicle proving grounds in Southern California.

Building Efficiency - Researchers in the MPC Lab also apply model predictive control theory to increase system efficiency in cooling or heating buildings.

Popular Recent Publications:

Semiautonomous Vehicular Control Using Driver Modeling (2014)

Stochastic Predictive Control for Semi-Autonomous Vehicles with an Uncertain Driver Model (2013)

Model Predictive Control for the Operation of Building Cooling Systems (2012)
